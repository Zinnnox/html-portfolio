The Best Movies According to Critics

Best movies of all time based on critics' picks, from variety.com

Psycho (1960)

"It’s a movie that speaks to us now more than ever, because it shows us, in every teasingly sinister moment, how life itself came to feel like a fun house poised over an abyss."

The Wizard of Oz (1939)

Victor Fleming’s joyous Technicolor classic, "The Wizard of Oz," has stood the test of time, enchanting multiple generations with its magical storytelling and vibrant visuals. The film's shift from black and white to color sets up audiences for a magical experience, making it a gold standard for cinematic enchantment.

The Godfather (1972)

Francis Ford Coppola's "The Godfather" is a classical epic that has embedded itself in popular culture, portraying the saga of the Corleone family with dark grandeur. The film's powerful depiction of organized crime and its deeply personal family drama make it a timeless masterpiece.
